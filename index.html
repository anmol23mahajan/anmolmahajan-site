The stranger who asked me how I really was doing

I was at a coffee shop on a Tuesday afternoon — the kind of unremarkable afternoon that usually leaves no trace. I ordered my usual, sat down, and a man at the next table asked me how I was doing. Not the polite, pass-through kind. He waited. He actually wanted to know.

I don't know why I answered honestly. Maybe because he wasn't anyone I'd need to impress. I told him I was a bit lost lately — not dramatically, just quietly. In that low-hum way where everything is technically fine but something feels slightly off-course.

"It's okay to be in the middle of something. Not every chapter has a title yet."

He said something I've been carrying since. He told me that the most interesting periods of his life always looked like confusion from the inside. That clarity is almost always a retrospective thing — you rarely see it while you're in it.

I've been thinking about that ever since. About how we're often waiting to feel certain before we move — and how certainty almost never comes first.

Hi — I'm Anmol. I started this site because I kept noticing things during the week and having nowhere to put them. Not big revelations. Just small observations that felt worth holding onto.

Every week I write about the one thing — a conversation, a moment, a thought — that didn't leave me. Sometimes it's something that happened. Sometimes it's something I finally understood. Occasionally it's something I'm still figuring out.

I try to write the way I'd talk to someone I trust: directly, without performing. No expert hat, no brand voice. Just a person thinking out loud, in public, every Sunday.

Why weekly?

Because a week is long enough for something to matter, and short enough to stay honest. The rhythm forces me to pay attention — and attention, I've found, is most of the work.
